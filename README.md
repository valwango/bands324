wripbands is my lil passion project of getting to design and flex my coding muscles again. and also work on something that i love purely.

the concept was birthed in 2022 while i was at usc in prof elizabeth ramsey's emotion in digital culture class. it was lightly explored in my undergraduate thesis, and now has finally come to life after needing a break from my post-grad working email beep boop career.

i keep every wristband i get at a concert, festival, or misc event. the inner tab that loops against your wrist on a paper/tyvek wristband is the sweet spot to yank on until the sticky rips off, and i use a black fine point sharpie to write the artist, venue, and date on the back of the band. if i'm in LA, it's thrown it in an incredibly overfilled carhartt paper shopping bag from paris that started as my memory collection site when i studied abroad the semester after taking prof. ramsey's class. if i'm away, it goes into the same exterior pocket in my carry-on suitcase before it makes it into that paper bag.

im currently in the process of emptying the bag and putting everything into binders and scrapbooks for my children and grandchildren and professional archivists to one day flip through, and it leaves me thinking of all of the shows that i went to that gave me a stamp on my hand or nothing at all where i'm missing the ephemera to look back on. i feel empty without a wristband. i don't care if the wristband is only for the bar and i have no intention of drinking. i'm pulling out my id and getting that wristband. why pay for a concert ticket when no wristband? what's the point? 

ive attempted to keep journals in the past where i write down every concert i've been to, and i know a lot of friends who keep their list in their notes app. creating wripbands was my combination of the two, the ability to elaborate and stack details like you would in a journal while keeping the simplicity and organization of a notes app list. 

my dad has this birthday app on his phone to track and notify him when a family member's birthday is approaching. around the time when i first started my job and was doing music market research on tiktok, i saw a screenshot of the countdown app for viewing what concerts that person had coming up in the next couple months. both of these apps were huge inspirations and references for the upcoming bands section's function and for the overall flow of the bands page. 

the design for what you see now on the site started in feb 2025, and the coding process has been going since then. 

the priority was making a site that fit all of my demands as a concertgoer, bc i feel like im pretty picky. now that this site is no longer just my own personal log and i am now sharing it with other people, it important for me to take other users' feedback into account too. my instagram is /valeriewango i'm all ears, please do share any and every thought about wripbands with me and i'd love to make this the sickest website that's ever existed on the world wide web.
